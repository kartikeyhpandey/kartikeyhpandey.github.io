My name is Kartikey, and I am a second-year computer engineering major at the University of Cincinnati. Upon completion of my degree, I plan to go to a business school and earn an MBA. I hope to work in the tech industry in the future. I am really fond of solving problems, that's how I developed an affinity for computer science and mathematics. However, my passion for solving problems goes beyond topics in school, I aim to contribute to my community by acknowledging the problems it faces and finding solutions. To talk about my college experience, I really enjoyed my first semester at UC. ​In the very first week of the fall semester, I started working in the Department of Chemical and Environmental Engineering as a web-developer; there I meet a lot of professors every day. Almost every day, I learn something new through so many interesting conversations with them about environmental problems plaguing our planet right now and their possible solutions. I love talking to people about their views on various topics; conversations like these help me to develop better thinking and see the world with a broader perspective. Presently, I have two leadership positions in a student organization called UC CubeCats. CubeCats aims to be the center of research and development in the space industry for university students. I am the secretary of the club and the Software & Payload Team Lead of Project Sphynx. Project Sphynx’s main objective is to build a high altitude balloon to measure the number of muon events per second at an altitude of about 95,000 feet. I got involved in UC CubeCats because they work towards solving prevalent problems in the Ohio area with the help of cube satellites.
